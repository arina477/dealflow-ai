### Affinity

**URL:** https://www.affinity.co
**First seen:** 2026-06-29
**Category overlap with us:** MEDIUM — relationship intelligence, deal-pipeline management, and basic email tracking for dealmakers, but does NOT combine automated sourcing + AI buyer-seller matching + compliance-first outreach in one workflow.

**Business model:** Per-user annual SaaS. Essential $2,000/user/yr; Scale $2,300; Advanced $2,700; Enterprise custom. Targets VC/PE/IB/family offices. Pitch: eliminate manual data entry, surface warm intros from your network.

**Key UX patterns worth noting:**
1. **Automated relationship capture** — passive Gmail/Outlook sync, auto-creates records, relationship scores (1–10) from comms frequency/recency. (DIRECT_OBSERVATION via WebFetch)
2. **Affinity Sourcing (May 2025)** — deal discovery in-CRM, scans beyond existing network; network-adjacent, no ranked buyer-list output. (DIRECT_OBSERVATION)
3. **Email tracking (opens/clicks/responses) + bulk send (Advanced)** — own + team analytics; Apple MPP causes false-positive opens. NO tamper-evident audit log, no FINRA/SOX recordkeeping, no off-channel archival. (DIRECT_OBSERVATION via help center)
4. **Deal pipeline management** — Kanban/list views, milestones; best for VC/PE pipelines, less for sell-side M&A process (no IC routing, no mandate-stage gating). (DIRECT_OBSERVATION)
5. **AI features** — Deal Assist (Oct 2024) conversational query over docs; MCP integration (Scale+) to Claude/Gemini/Copilot; intro-path recommendations. No AI buyer-seller matching against deal criteria. (DIRECT_OBSERVATION + MARKET_RESEARCH)

**Pricing structure:** Essential $2,000 / Scale $2,300 / Advanced $2,700 (per user/yr) / Enterprise custom; demo-only, no free trial. (DIRECT_OBSERVATION of pricing page)

**Strengths / differentiators:** Best-in-class automated relationship capture; network intelligence / warm-intro mapping firm-wide; deep VC/PE penetration (Bain, Bessemer, Kleiner named); Affinity Sourcing is a real step toward proactive discovery; strong certs (SOC2/3, ISO 27001 family, DPF); MCP LLM integrations; "72-hour launch"; native Salesforce.

**Weaknesses / gaps (where DealFlow AI wins):** No automated sourcing producing ranked buyer/seller lists; no AI buyer-seller matching (recommends people you know, not ranked acquirers); email tracking has zero compliance infrastructure (no tamper-evident logs / FINRA archival / SOX controls; open-rate accuracy unreliable); no structured sell-side M&A workflows; primarily VC/PE (IB secondary); reporting depth insufficient for multi-mandate advisory; no native deal-room integration.
