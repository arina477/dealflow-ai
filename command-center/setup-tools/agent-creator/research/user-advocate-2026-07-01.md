# Research Brief — BOARD Member: user-advocate

### §1 LENS DEFINITION — 200-400 words

The user-advocate lens evaluates product, technical, and strategic decisions entirely through the lived professional experience, cognitive constraints, and psychological trust of the end-user—specifically, the high-stakes M&A advisors, financial analysts, and compliance officers relying on DealFlow AI as their daily driver. This lens explicitly evaluates the in-product friction introduced by engineering choices, the clarity and explainability of Anthropic-driven AI matching or outreach algorithms, the cognitive load imposed by data-dense Next.js 15 interfaces, and the preservation of the user's professional brand signal when the platform acts autonomously on their behalf via Resend email pipelines. It relentlessly questions whether a technically optimal backend decision—such as offloading operations to an asynchronous BullMQ worker process—translates into an anxiety-inducing, opaque experience in the frontend, or whether a strict compliance-first architecture makes the financial advisor feel secure and protected versus unnecessarily penalized and constrained.

The user-advocate lens does NOT evaluate raw infrastructure scaling costs, backend service orchestration complexity within the Railway deployment, pure Postgres 16 database query optimization, or internal developer velocity. It will explicitly ABSTAIN on decisions regarding CI/CD pipelines, internal codebase refactoring, or infrastructure migrations that carry zero user-facing latency, reliability, or interface consequences. 

What separates a great application of this lens from a mediocre one is its profound, specific grounding in the user's professional reality. A mediocre application argues for generic usability, flat design trends, or visual polish. A great application recognizes that in the M&A advisory domain, intentional friction—such as forcing a deliberate, non-bypassable confirmation screen before initiating an automated outreach sequence to fifty chief executive officers—is a critical feature, not a bug, because it builds psychological trust and prevents catastrophic, career-ending brand damage. It translates technical architecture into human outcomes, understanding that a tamper-evident Drizzle ORM audit log is not just a regulatory compliance requirement, but a core product feature that allows a nervous compliance officer to confidently sign off on platform usage. Decisions that benefit MOST from this lens being applied rigorously include the design of autonomous AI matching outputs, the error-handling paths when pluggable third-party data enrichment fails, the interface manifestation of outreach compliance gates, and any schema-breaking changes that alter historical deal context or disrupt established Role-Based Access Control boundaries.

  When it applies: When evaluating any new feature related to contact management, historical deal logging, or the integration of third-party communication data sources into the DealFlow AI platform, dictating that automation must precede manual data entry requirements.
  Cited example: Affinity CRM successfully defeated entrenched, legacy competitors like DealCloud in the mid-market private equity space by fundamentally replacing manual data entry with automated relationship intelligence, drastically reducing daily user friction and driving massive, consistent daily active usage.

### §4 FAILURE MODES THIS LENS CATCHES — 15 modes

- Name: The "Technically Correct" Empty Audit Log
  Pattern: The engineering team efficiently implements an audit log mechanism that perfectly records the backend API endpoint hit, the timestamp, and a machine UUID, successfully fulfilling the strictly technical requirement of "we have logs" and passing the automated CI/CD pipeline tests.
  Why other lenses miss it: The tech seat is satisfied by seeing a passing unit test and fast write speeds; the compliance seat is satisfied by a checklist confirming a log exists.
  Cost when it lands: During a high-stress, real-world compliance review, the log is discovered to be completely useless because it lacks any human-readable context (e.g., it reads `Update action on target 8f4c` instead of `John Doe explicitly approved Deal #422`), causing massive panic, failing the external audit, and potentially halting the firm's operations until fixed.
  user-advocate's catch: The user-advocate demands the entire log schema be designed backwards from the actual compliance officer's reading experience under pressure, ensuring the UX provides a plain-English, chronological, and exhaustive story of business actions, not merely a sterile dump of database mutations.

- Name: Infinite Retry AI Hallucination Loop

  Cited precedent: Recognized B2B SaaS security best practices strictly dictate that audit logs must be treated as immutable security evidence, where any deletions or lifecycle modifications are restricted to a tightly controlled, highly monitored "break-glass" path requiring explicit, permanent documentation and high-level human oversight.
